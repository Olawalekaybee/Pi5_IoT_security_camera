Known limitations

Overclaiming undermines a project meant to demonstrate engineering judgment, not just feature count.

Not anti-spoofing hardware

The liveness check is a motion heuristic. It catches a held-up photo or phone screen but won't reliably catch a tripod-mounted photo or a sophisticated replay attack — real liveness detection needs depth or IR sensing this Pi doesn't have.

Enrollment quality matters

A single low-quality enrollment photo measurably hurts Re-ID match confidence. Multi-photo enrollment from the dashboard exists specifically to mitigate this, not eliminate it.

Dev server, not production WSGI

Flask's built-in server is adequate at this scale — one camera, a handful of viewers — but a heavier deployment would warrant Gunicorn with gevent workers for concurrent MJPEG and SSE streaming.
